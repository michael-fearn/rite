# Fortress-owned Share reconciliation

Status: ready-for-agent
Category: enhancement

## What to build

Extend NAS Reconcile from read-only planning to API-backed writes for fortress-owned derived NFS Shares. Reconcile may create, update, or destroy only Shares carrying a durable fortress ownership marker. Mount removals, Mount access changes, and Mount point changes require operator confirmation before reconciliation continues.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Created NFS Shares carry a durable fortress ownership marker and deterministic identity.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile updates only fortress-owned Shares and refuses to mutate unmanaged Shares.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile destroys stale fortress-owned Shares only when no VM or Service declaration still requires them.
- [ ] Mount removal, `access` change, and `mount_point` change are surfaced in preflight output and require explicit operator confirmation.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile does not roll back Shares after downstream VM Configure or Service deployment failures.
- [ ] Tests cover create, update, stale delete, unmanaged conflict refusal, and confirmation-gated changes.

## Agent Brief

**Category:** enhancement
**Summary:** Extend NAS Reconcile from read-only planning to API-backed writes for fortress-owned derived NFS Shares.

**Current behavior:**
NAS Reconcile can load Inventory, derive desired NFS Shares from VM Mount and Service consumption declarations, compare that intent with TrueNAS reality, and emit a read-only plan. The plan validates adopted Dataset existence and owner UID/GID, reports missing desired Shares, reports stale fortress-owned Shares, and blocks when unmanaged Shares overlap desired fortress-owned Share intent.

The read-only plan deliberately does not create, update, or delete TrueNAS Shares. It also does not yet implement durable ownership marker creation, API-backed mutation, stale Share deletion, or operator confirmation for Mount removal, `access` changes, or `mount_point` changes.

**Desired behavior:**
NAS Reconcile should be able to apply Share changes through the TrueNAS API, but only for fortress-owned derived NFS Shares. Created Shares must have deterministic identity and a durable fortress ownership marker that future reconciliations can detect. Existing Shares without that marker are unmanaged and must not be mutated or deleted by fortress.

When desired Share intent is missing, NAS Reconcile should create the corresponding NFS Share with the correct Dataset path, access policy, client list, deterministic name, and fortress ownership marker. When an existing fortress-owned Share differs from desired intent, NAS Reconcile should update only fortress-owned fields needed to match the derived intent. When a fortress-owned Share is stale, NAS Reconcile may delete it only after proving no VM Mount or Service declaration still requires it.

Potentially disruptive Mount changes need an explicit operator confirmation gate before reconciliation continues. At minimum, removing a declared Mount, changing a Mount's `access`, or changing a Mount's `mount_point` should appear in preflight output and require explicit confirmation. NAS Reconcile should not roll back Share mutations if later VM Configure or Service deployment work fails; later workflows should report their own failures without undoing the already-applied NAS state.

**Key interfaces:**
- NAS Reconcile operator workflow — should support an apply/write mode in addition to read-only planning.
- NAS reality reader/client abstraction — should grow the ability to create, update, and delete NFS Shares while keeping credentials out of logs, errors, and plan output.
- Desired NFS Share model — should continue to derive deterministic Share identity from Dataset, protocol, and compatible Access Policies, independent of VM-local Mount Name.
- Fortress ownership marker — durable TrueNAS-side metadata or representation that distinguishes fortress-owned Shares from unmanaged Shares across runs.
- Preflight/confirmation model — should represent Mount removals, Mount `access` changes, and Mount `mount_point` changes before writes proceed.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Created NFS Shares carry a durable fortress ownership marker and deterministic identity.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile updates only fortress-owned Shares and refuses to mutate unmanaged Shares.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile creates missing fortress-owned NFS Shares with derived Dataset path, protocol, access policy, and VM client IPs.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile updates drifted fortress-owned NFS Shares to match derived intent.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile destroys stale fortress-owned Shares only when no VM Mount or Service declaration still requires them.
- [ ] Mount removal, `access` change, and `mount_point` change are surfaced in preflight output and require explicit operator confirmation.
- [ ] NAS Reconcile does not roll back Shares after downstream VM Configure or Service deployment failures.
- [ ] Tests cover create, update, stale delete, unmanaged conflict refusal, and confirmation-gated changes.
- [ ] Secrets and TrueNAS credentials remain redacted from logs, errors, and plan/apply output.

**Out of scope:**
- Creating, deleting, adopting, or repairing ordinary adopted Datasets.
- Mutating unmanaged or manually-created TrueNAS Shares.
- Adopting existing manual Shares into fortress ownership.
- Implementing Ephemeral Dataset acceptance-test support.
- Rolling back NAS Share writes after later VM Configure or Service deployment failures.
